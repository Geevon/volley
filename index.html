About Volleyball

Volleyball is a fun, social, and active sport that anyone can learn! Whether you're looking for a new hobby, a way to stay fit, or just want to try something new, volleyball is a great choice for beginners of all ages.

This site is designed to help absolute beginners learn the fundamentals of volleyball in a friendly, accessible way. We'll break down the basics, help you develop skills, and connect you with resources to start playing.

Volleyball Basics

The Court

A volleyball court is 18m x 9m, divided by a net. The height of the net is 2.43m for men and 2.24m for women.

The Game

Two teams of six players try to score points by grounding the ball on the opponent's court. Each team can touch the ball up to three times before sending it over the net.

Scoring

Modern volleyball uses rally scoring - a point is scored on every play regardless of which team served. Games are typically played to 25 points (win by 2).

Simple Rules to Get Started

  • Each team gets up to three touches to return the ball over the net
  • Players cannot touch the net during play
  • The ball must be clearly hit, not caught or thrown
  • Players can't hit the ball twice in succession (except when blocking)
  • The ball can touch the net while crossing over
  • A ball landing on the line is considered "in"

Ready to Get Started?

Equipment Needed

  • Comfortable athletic clothes
  • Indoor court shoes
  • Knee pads (recommended)
  • A volleyball (for practice)

Find a Place to Play

  • Local community centers
  • School gyms
  • Volleyball clubs
  • Beach volleyball courts
  • Recreational leagues

Practice Tips

  • Start with passing against a wall
  • Practice serving from a short distance
  • Watch tutorial videos online
  • Join a beginners clinic
  • Find a friend to practice with
