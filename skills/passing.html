Passing Fundamentals

Learn How to Pass in Volleyball

Passing, also called "receiving," is the first touch after the serve and the foundation of a strong team play. Good passing gives your team the chance to set up an attack!

Types of Passes

  • Forearm Pass (Bump): Most common way to receive a serve or a hard-hit ball.
  • Overhead Pass: Used for balls coming slow and high; similar to setting.

Steps for a Proper Forearm Pass

  1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and knees slightly bent.
  2. Hold your hands together to form a flat platform with your forearms.
  3. Keep your arms straight and use your legs to lift the ball, not your arms.
  4. Face the ball and angle your platform toward your target.
