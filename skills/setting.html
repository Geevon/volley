Setting Fundamentals

Learn How to Set in Volleyball

Setting is the "assist" of volleyball — it's about giving your hitter the perfect ball to spike. If passing is the "foundation," setting is the architect behind every great play!

Types of Sets

  • High Set: A high, slow ball — perfect for beginners and easy for hitters to spike.
  • Quick Set: A fast, low ball to catch the defense off-guard.
  • Back Set: A tricky toss over your head behind you, used to surprise blockers.

Steps for a Proper Volleyball Set

  1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, knees bent, ready to move.
  2. Raise your hands above your forehead, creating a triangle shape with your thumbs and fingers.
  3. Contact the ball with your fingertips (NOT the palms!) to control it.
  4. Extend your arms and legs together as you push the ball upward.
  5. Follow through with your hands toward your target — think "smooth and soft."
